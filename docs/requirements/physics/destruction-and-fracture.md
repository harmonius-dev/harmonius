# R-4.6 — Destruction & Fracture Requirements

## R-4.6.1 Voronoi Fracture Generation

The engine **SHALL** generate 3D Voronoi fracture assets at build time that produce convex hull
fragments whose combined volume differs from the original mesh volume by less than 1%.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.1](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Volume-preserving fracture prevents visible gaps or overlaps between fragments
  when destruction activates at runtime.
- **Verification:** Unit test — fracture a unit cube into 20 Voronoi fragments; sum the convex
  hull volumes of all fragments; assert the total is within 1% of the original cube volume.

## R-4.6.2 Pre-Fractured Mesh Authoring

The engine **SHALL** load fracture assets authored in DCC tools or generated by the content
pipeline, and spawn fragment entities with `RigidBody`, `Collider`, and `DebrisLifetime`
components plus joint entities connecting adjacent fragments within one frame of fracture
activation.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.2](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Art-directed destruction for hero objects (castle walls, bridges) requires
  designer-authored fracture patterns loaded from pre-built assets.
- **Verification:** Integration test — load a pre-fractured asset with 15 fragments; trigger
  fracture; assert all 15 fragment entities and their adjacency joint entities are spawned
  within one frame; verify each fragment has `RigidBody`, `Collider`, and `DebrisLifetime`
  components.

## R-4.6.3 Runtime Fracture and Activation

The engine **SHALL** trigger fracture on a `Destructible` entity within one frame of its
`DamageHealth` exceeding the damage threshold, despawning the intact entity and spawning all
fragment entities with physics components initialized from the fracture asset.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.3](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Destruction must be responsive to gameplay damage events; delays cause visible
  desyncs between damage effects and physical breakup.
- **Verification:** Integration test — apply damage exceeding the threshold to a `Destructible`
  entity; assert the intact entity is despawned and all fragment entities are spawned within the
  same frame; verify fragment `Transform` positions match the fracture asset layout.

## R-4.6.4 Progressive Damage Model

The engine **SHALL** decrease the `DamageHealth` integrity value by an amount proportional to
contact impulse magnitude, and transition through at least two visual damage stages before full
fracture.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.4](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Progressive damage provides gameplay feedback (cracks, deformation) before
  destruction, letting players gauge structural integrity.
- **Verification:** Integration test — apply incremental impulses to a `DamageHealth` entity
  configured with 3 damage stages; assert each stage threshold triggers in order; verify the
  integrity value decreases proportionally to impulse magnitude within 5% tolerance.

## R-4.6.5 Stress Propagation and Structural Collapse

The engine **SHALL** detect unsupported fragments (no path to a grounded anchor in the joint
connectivity graph) within one frame of a load-bearing joint breaking, and release those
fragments to fall under gravity.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.5](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Cascading structural collapse (arches, buildings, bridges) is a key destruction
  feature that depends on correct graph connectivity analysis.
- **Verification:** Integration test — build a 3-column arch from fragment entities connected by
  joints, with outer columns grounded; break the keystone joint; assert all unsupported
  fragments above the break have their joint entities despawned within one frame and begin
  falling under gravity.

## R-4.6.6 Debris Simulation and Lifecycle

The engine **SHALL** despawn debris entities whose `DebrisLifetime` timer has expired, and
enforce a global maximum debris count by despawning the oldest debris entities first when the
cap is exceeded.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.6](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Unbounded debris accumulation causes memory and performance degradation;
  lifecycle management keeps the simulation within budget.
- **Verification:** Stress test — spawn 500 debris entities with 5-second lifetimes and a cap
  of 200; assert the total active debris count never exceeds 200; assert all entities are
  despawned within 1 frame of their lifetime expiring.

## R-4.6.7 Debris Pooling and LOD

The engine **SHALL** recycle despawned debris entities via an object pool, and reduce collision
shape complexity for debris beyond the configured LOD distance, removing `RigidBody` and
`Collider` components entirely for debris beyond the maximum simulation distance.

- **Derived from:** [F-4.6.7](../../features/physics/destruction-and-fracture.md)
- **Rationale:** Entity allocation churn during destruction events causes frame hitches; pooling
  and LOD keep simulation cost proportional to nearby debris.
- **Verification:** Benchmark — trigger 10 successive destruction events spawning 50 fragments
  each with pooling enabled vs. disabled; measure peak frame time and allocation count; assert
  pooling reduces allocation count by at least 80%. Integration test — verify debris beyond the
  LOD distance has no `RigidBody` or `Collider` components.

## Non-Functional Requirements

### R-4.6.NF1 Fracture Activation Frame Budget

Fracture activation (despawning the intact entity and spawning all fragment entities with
physics components) **SHALL** complete within 2 ms for objects with up to 50 fragments, and
budget spawning across frames for objects exceeding 50 fragments.

- **Derived from:** R-4.6.3, R-4.6.2
- **Rationale:** Destruction events during gameplay must not cause visible frame hitches; large
  fractures must be amortized across frames to stay within budget.
- **Verification:** Benchmark — trigger fracture on an object with 50 fragments; measure
  wall-clock time for the activation frame; assert it completes within 2 ms.

### R-4.6.NF2 Maximum Active Debris Count

The engine **SHALL** enforce a configurable global debris cap (default 500) and maintain
stable frame rates when the cap is reached during sustained destruction sequences.

- **Derived from:** R-4.6.6, R-4.6.7
- **Rationale:** Sustained battles can generate thousands of fragments; an enforced cap
  prevents unbounded memory and simulation cost growth.
- **Verification:** Stress test — trigger continuous destruction generating 2,000 fragments
  with a cap of 500; assert the active debris count never exceeds 500; measure frame time
  and assert it remains within the physics budget.

### R-4.6.NF3 Structural Analysis Scalability

Stress propagation graph traversal **SHALL** complete within 0.5 ms for connectivity graphs
containing up to 200 fragment nodes.

- **Derived from:** R-4.6.5
- **Rationale:** Structural collapse analysis runs every frame a load-bearing joint breaks;
  it must be fast enough to avoid frame spikes during cascade events.
- **Verification:** Benchmark — build a 200-node fragment connectivity graph; break a central
  load-bearing node; measure graph traversal time; assert it completes within 0.5 ms.
