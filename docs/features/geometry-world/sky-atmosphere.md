# 3.5 — Sky & Atmosphere

## Procedural Sky

### F-3.5.1 Procedural Sky Model

A physically-motivated analytical sky model (Preetham/Hosek-Wilkie) provides fast sky color evaluation for any sun
position. The model computes sky luminance and chromaticity as a function of view angle, sun zenith, and
atmospheric turbidity. It serves as the fallback sky representation when volumetric atmosphere scattering is
budget-limited, and as the reference for baking sky lookup tables.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.1
- **Dependencies:** None
- **Platform notes:** None

## Physically-Based Atmosphere Scattering

### F-3.5.2 Multi-Scattering Atmosphere with Aerial Perspective

A precomputed atmosphere scattering model based on Bruneton's improved method. Rayleigh scattering, Mie
scattering, and ozone absorption are integrated along view rays using precomputed transmittance, single-scattering,
and multi-scattering lookup tables. The LUTs are recomputed when atmosphere parameters change. Aerial perspective
is applied to all scene geometry via a froxel-based volume, fading distant terrain and structures into atmospheric
haze naturally. This is critical for MMO draw distances spanning tens of kilometers.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.2
- **Dependencies:** None
- **Platform notes:** None

## Volumetric Clouds

### F-3.5.3 Ray-Marched Volumetric Clouds

Clouds are rendered by ray marching through a cloud volume defined by layered noise fields (Perlin-Worley for
shape, Worley for detail erosion). Cloud density, coverage, and type are controlled by a weather map texture
that can be animated or driven by a weather simulation. Lighting uses a cone-sampled in-scattering approach with
multi-octave extinction and ambient sky contribution. Temporal reprojection across multiple frames amortizes the
per-pixel ray-march cost.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.3
- **Dependencies:** F-3.5.2
- **Platform notes:** None

### F-3.5.4 Cloud Shadow Map

Cloud coverage is projected onto the terrain and scene as a shadow map generated by rendering the cloud density
field from the sun's perspective. This map modulates direct lighting on terrain, foliage, and water, producing
large-scale moving shadow patterns across the open world that reinforce the sense of dynamic weather and scale.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.4
- **Dependencies:** F-3.5.3
- **Platform notes:** None

## Dynamic Time of Day

### F-3.5.5 Dynamic Time-of-Day System

A time-of-day controller drives sun and moon positions along astronomically-derived arcs, continuously updating sky
colors, atmosphere LUTs, ambient light intensity, shadow direction, and fog parameters. Transitions through dawn,
day, dusk, and night are smooth and physically motivated. The system exposes a time scale parameter for gameplay
control, supporting accelerated day/night cycles common in MMO worlds.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.5
- **Dependencies:** F-3.5.2, F-3.5.3
- **Platform notes:** None

## Celestial Bodies

### F-3.5.6 Celestial Body Rendering

Sun, moon, stars, and planetary bodies are rendered as part of the sky dome. The sun is a bright disc with
configurable limb darkening. The moon uses a textured disc with phase-accurate illumination. Stars are rendered
from a catalog with magnitude-based brightness, twinkling, and atmospheric extinction near the horizon. All
celestial positions are astronomically computed or artist-overridden.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.6
- **Dependencies:** F-3.5.5
- **Platform notes:** None

## Sky Capture

### F-3.5.7 Environment Cubemap Capture

The sky, atmosphere, and cloud systems are captured into a low-resolution environment cubemap each frame or at
reduced update rates. This cubemap provides ambient diffuse and specular lighting for the entire scene via
image-based lighting (IBL). Capture is performed by rendering the sky shader into cubemap faces on a round-robin
schedule to amortize cost, ensuring reflections and ambient lighting always match the current sky state.

- **Requirements:** R-3.5.7
- **Dependencies:** F-3.5.2, F-3.5.3, F-3.5.6
- **Platform notes:** None
